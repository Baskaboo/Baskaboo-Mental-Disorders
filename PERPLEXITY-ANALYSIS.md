## Perplexity Analysis: Baskaboo–Mental-Disorders and Predictive Coding

This repository investigates how **Baskaboo’s “Input/Output Portal” model (Pits/Laram)** converges with **contemporary theories of Predictive Coding in neuroscience**.  
Instead of diagnosing mental disorders, this project explores how **Baskaboo’s “Information Decryption” model** can provide a **non‑clinical, narrative lens** for understanding neurodiversity.

### Key Insight
- In Predictive Coding, the brain generates predictions about the world and updates these models based on prediction errors.  
- Baskaboo interprets **Pits/Laram as an Input/Output Portal** — a dynamic gateway that:
  - filters incoming data (Pits → Energy → Information),  
  - and encodes outgoing experience (Laram → Memory → Time/Reality).

This implies that **neurodivergent** or **unfiltered states** — such as “unfiltered data streams” in the “Schizophrenia Firewall Breach” — could be understood as:
- situations where the **portal’s filters** are overwhelmed or bypassed,  
- leading to a **high‑entropy information state** similar to those described in **quantum cognition** and **information‑theoretic models** of the mind.

### Why This Matters for Neurodiversity
- The idea of **“Information Decryption” instead of “Scientific Diagnosis”**:
  - shifts focus from labels to **patterns of experience**,  
  - and encourages a **holistic, self‑reflective** approach.
- Baskaboo‑Mental‑Disorders uses this framework to:
  - help users explore how their internal “Pits/Laram flow” affects perception,  
  - and to foster a deeper understanding of neurodiversity without stigmatization.
